Mobile CMMS: Why Your Techs Need Smartphones on the Floor

January 30, 2026 • 6 min read • By Mike Harper

Your maintenance techs spend 25-35% of their time walking. Walking to the office to get work orders. Walking back to log completions. Walking to find manuals, schematics, parts lists.

A smartphone in their pocket eliminates most of that waste.

The Paper Problem

TaskPaper-BasedMobile CMMS
Get work orderWalk to office (5 min)Push notification (0 min)
Find schematicHunt through binders (10 min)Search on phone (30 sec)
Log completionWalk back, fill form (10 min)Tap "complete" (10 sec)
Order partsEmail or call (15 min)Scan and request (1 min)

Real Results

  • 25-30% increase in wrench time (time actually fixing equipment)
  • 40% reduction in time to close work orders
  • Real-time visibility for managers (no chasing techs for updates)
  • Better data capture (photos, notes, timestamps)

Common Objections (and Answers)

  • "Techs will waste time on phones" — Usage logs show they're in the CMMS app, not social media
  • "It's too complicated" — Modern CMMS apps are easier than using a calculator
  • "What about dirty hands?" — Voice input and rugged cases exist
