What Recovery Requires

Analysts estimate Venezuela needs $100 billion in investment to rebuild its oil industry. But money alone isn't enough. Recovery requires:

1. Workovers and Repairs

Most near-term production gains will come from rehabilitating existing wells, not drilling new ones. This means:

  • Well intervention services
  • Pump replacements and repairs
  • Pipeline integrity work
  • Control system updates

2. Knowledge Recovery

Much institutional knowledge was lost with departed workers. Rebuilding requires:

  • Recruiting returned diaspora workers
  • Training new technicians quickly
  • Capturing knowledge from remaining experts
  • AI-powered knowledge management systems

3. Modern Technology Layer

1990s-era control systems can still function, but they need:

  • Protocol gateways for modern connectivity
  • Data collection for AI analysis
  • Mobile tools for field technicians
  • Diagnostic assistance systems

Risk Factors

Important caveats for anyone considering Venezuela engagement:

  • Political Risk: The current situation remains fluid. Reforms could be reversed.
  • Sanctions Compliance: US sanctions have been eased but not eliminated. Legal review is essential.
  • Payment/Currency: Venezuelan bolivar is unstable. USD mechanisms needed.
  • Security: Some areas have security concerns. Travel requires planning.

The opportunity is real, but so are the risks. Due diligence is essential.
