CMMS Implementation: A No-BS Guide for SMB Manufacturers

January 30, 2026 • 10 min read • By Mike Harper

Most CMMS implementations fail. Not because the software is bad, but because companies try to boil the ocean. Here's how to actually succeed.

The 4-Week Implementation

Week 1: Foundation

  • List your 20 most critical assets
  • Set up locations/areas
  • Add your maintenance team as users
  • Don't touch PM schedules yet

Week 2: Work Orders

  • Start logging reactive work orders
  • Keep it simple: What, Where, Who, When
  • Train techs on mobile app
  • Celebrate first 10 completed WOs

Week 3: History Building

  • Continue logging all work
  • Add photos to work orders
  • Start noticing patterns
  • Add 5 more critical assets

Week 4: PM Schedules

  • Add PM tasks for top 10 assets only
  • Start with what you already do
  • Don't add new PMs yet — just digitize existing
  • Review and adjust

Common Mistakes

  • Too much too fast: Don't add 500 assets on day 1
  • Perfect data syndrome: Start messy, clean up later
  • Ignoring techs: They have to use it — involve them early
  • No executive sponsor: Someone needs to enforce usage

Success Metrics

  • Week 1: System configured, users trained
  • Week 4: 80% of work orders logged in system
  • Week 8: PM compliance > 90%
  • Week 12: Measurable reduction in downtime
