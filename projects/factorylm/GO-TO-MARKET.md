# FactoryLM Go-To-Market: AR Glasses First

## Positioning

**Not a CMMS. Not a chatbot. An AR platform for industrial intelligence.**

> "FactoryLM puts AI-powered diagnostics in your line of sight. Point. Ask. Fix."

## The Insight

Every industrial AR play is enterprise-first:
- RealWear: $1,200+ headset, enterprise sales cycle
- Microsoft HoloLens: $3,500+, requires IT integration
- Magic Leap: $3,299, developer-focused
- Google Glass Enterprise: Discontinued

**The gap:** SMB industrial companies (1-50 employees) have zero AR options.

FactoryLM fills this gap with:
- $349 hardware (Frame glasses)
- $99/month software
- No IT department required
- Works out of the box

## Target Customer

### Primary: Independent HVAC/Industrial Contractors
- 1-10 technicians
- $500K-$5M annual revenue
- Currently uses: paper, basic CMMS, phone calls
- Pain: Senior techs retiring, knowledge walking out the door
- Budget: Can spend $500-1000/month on tools

### Secondary: Small Manufacturing Plants
- 10-50 maintenance staff
- Own facility, own equipment
- Pain: Downtime costs $10K+/hour
- Budget: Will pay for anything that reduces downtime

### Tertiary: Training/Education
- Trade schools
- Corporate training departments
- Pain: Can't scale expert instruction
- Budget: Per-seat licensing

## Go-To-Market Phases

### Phase 1: Founder-Led Sales (Months 1-3)
**Goal:** 5 paying pilot customers

**Channels:**
- Mike's existing contractor network
- LinkedIn outreach to HVAC company owners
- Local trade association meetings
- Reddit r/HVAC, r/PLC, r/electricians

**Offer:**
- Free Frame glasses (you eat the $349)
- 3 months free software
- In exchange: weekly feedback calls, video testimonial

**Why this works:**
- Proves product-market fit
- Creates case study content
- Builds relationships for referrals

### Phase 2: Content-Led Growth (Months 3-6)
**Goal:** 50 paying customers

**Content Strategy:**

2. **LinkedIn:** Daily posts from Mike
   - Industrial maintenance tips
   - Behind-the-scenes of building FactoryLM
   - Customer success stories
   
3. **Trade Publications:**
   - HVACR Business

   - Maintenance Technology

**SEO Targets:**
- "AR glasses for maintenance"
- "AI industrial diagnostics"
- "hands-free troubleshooting"
- "[equipment name] fault code [code]" (long-tail)

### Phase 3: Channel Partners (Months 6-12)
**Goal:** 200 paying customers

**Partners:**
1. **Equipment Distributors**
   - Sell FactoryLM alongside equipment
   - Revenue share: 20-30%
   
2. **Training Companies**
   - License for courses
   - Per-seat model
   
3. **CMMS Vendors**
   - Integration partnerships
   - Referral fees

4. **Brilliant Labs**
   - Co-marketing with Frame
   - Featured in their case studies

### Phase 4: Expert Network Marketplace (Month 6+)
**Goal:** Create supply-side moat

**The Play:**
- Recruit 100+ industrial experts globally
- Philippines, India, Eastern Europe first
- Pay $64/hour (10x local wage)
- Charge customers $100/hour

**Why This Matters:**
- Expertise becomes a platform, not just software
- Network effects: more experts = better matching = happier customers
- Recurring revenue from support sessions
