## ðŸš¨ The Challenge

### Reactive Maintenance Crippling Production Efficiency

ACME's Texas facility was struggling with an outdated maintenance approach that relied heavily on reactive responses to equipment failures. With increasing production demands for their new EV battery line, unplanned downtime was becoming critically expensive.

**Pain Points:**
- **Unpredictable Equipment Failures:** Critical production equipment would fail without warning, causing cascade failures across interconnected systems
- **Information Overload:** Technicians received 200+ alarms daily with no prioritization, leading to critical issues being missed in the noise
- **Knowledge Gaps:** Retiring senior technicians were taking decades of troubleshooting expertise with them, leaving newer staff struggling with complex repairs

**Business Impact Before FactoryLM:**
- Average MTTR: 4.2 hours
- Alarm response time: 18 minutes average
- Technician efficiency: Only 60% of repairs completed on first attempt
- Annual maintenance costs: $8.5M including downtime losses
- Production line availability: 78% (well below industry benchmark of 85%+)
