**Title:** Hands-Free Maintenance is Finally Here

**Body:**

Picture this:

A technician climbs into a crane boom 80 feet in the air. Both hands on the ladder. Phone in their pocket. Manual back in the truck.

Something's wrong with the hydraulic valve. They need the torque spec. The wiring diagram. Maybe a video call with the expert back at the shop.

Old way: Climb down. Walk back. Climb up again. Repeat.

New way: "Hey glasses, show me the hydraulic schematic for this model."

Smart glasses aren't science fiction anymore. Field service teams are reporting:

→ 30-40% reduction in average repair time
→ Remote expert support without leaving the job site  
→ Real-time equipment data overlay while working
→ Hands free the entire time

TotalEnergies is using AR glasses for refinery maintenance. Automotive plants are completing line-side inspections without leaving the work zone.

The technology finally caught up with the promise.

And it's not just for giant enterprises. Consumer AR glasses are hitting sub-$500 price points with AI assistants built in.

The question isn't whether this will transform field maintenance.

It's whether you'll be early or late.
